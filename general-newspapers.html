Overview

This page tracks obituary data drawn from general newspaper digitization projects rather than focused aggregators. These sources often provide long historical runs, but with uneven coverage across states, newspapers, and time periods.

The goal here is to record what portals we are using, how we construct queries, and how we handle issues like OCR quality, missing issues, and misclassified content.

Portals

Candidate portals for Pennsylvania and Georgia (TBA):

— Chronicling America (Library of Congress).
— State-level digital newspaper programs or archives.
— University-based digitization projects that host local or regional newspapers.
— Commercial or subscription databases used for historical newspapers, if applicable.

As we lock in exact sources and access methods, we can list them here with links and brief notes.

Coverage & Gaps

This section is for summarizing where general newspaper sources provide robust obituary coverage and where we expect gaps, including:

— Periods before digitization starts or after it ends for key titles.
— Regions or demographic groups underrepresented in surviving runs.
— Newspaper types (e.g., small-town weeklies vs. metropolitan dailies) that may skew the kinds of obituaries we see.

These notes can feed back into the high-level “Obituary Data Overview” table and guide where targeted collection is most urgent.

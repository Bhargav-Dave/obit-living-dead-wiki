Overview

This component of the project covers obituary content drawn from “general” newspaper collections, primarily state-level archives and U.S. national digitization programs. The goal is to build a baseline view of how obituaries appear in local and regional print media over time, against which more specialized collections (e.g., African American funeral programs) can be compared.

Sources

Primary sources for general newspaper obituaries include:

  • Chronicling America (Library of Congress): https://www.loc.gov/collections/chronicling-america/
  • Georgia Historic Newspapers: https://gahistoricnewspapers.galileo.usg.edu/
  • Pennsylvania Newspaper Archive: https://panewsarchive.psu.edu/newspapers/
  • Other library and funeral home sources: to be added as we identify relevant digitized runs with obituary coverage for Pennsylvania and Georgia.

Text Scraping Methodology

For general newspapers, we scrape selected pages from carefully chosen titles rather than attempting a complete crawl of all available material. The selection focuses on:

  • Newspapers with good OCR quality and reasonably structured issue/edition metadata.
  • Titles with sustained coverage of obituaries or death notices.
  • Runs that provide meaningful overlap across the Pennsylvania–Georgia comparison.

Within those titles, we identify candidate pages for scraping using keyword filters. Pages are selected when they contain obituary-indicative terms (e.g., “obituary”, “died”, “funeral”, etc.) and do not contain excluded words that reliably indicate non-obituary content. These wordlists are maintained by collaborators in a shared Google document and are being updated over time.

Extracted page text is stored along with issue-level metadata (date, title, page number, and any section labels) so that downstream processes can decide which items qualify as obituary-like and how to code them.

The Living Dead

A project analyzing how obituaries across centuries reveal changing patterns of connection, inequality, and remembrance.

Pennsylvania & Georgia · 18th century–present
Obituaries as cultural texts Mixed methods LLMs & archives
Current focus
From raw pages to linked lives

We are building a long-run obituary dataset for two states, tracing who is remembered, how relationships are narrated, and how inequalities surface in public commemoration.

  • Refining obituary detection in historical newspapers
  • Designing qualitative coding for ties, values, and exclusions
  • Prototyping LLM pipelines for modernization and extraction

Context
Where

States & sites

Pennsylvania and Georgia were selected for their contrasting regional histories, sizable Black populations, and rich archival coverage in both state and national repositories.

When

Time span

Covering obituaries from the mid-18th century to the early 21st century, with attention to shifts in who appears, what is recorded, and how ties are framed.

How

Approach

Mixed-methods: iterative qualitative coding, statistical analysis, and computational pipelines (LLMs, NLP, network analysis) to link individual stories to broader social change.

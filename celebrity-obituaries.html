Overview

The celebrity obituary set provides a complementary perspective to local and regional obituaries. It focuses on nationally visible figures whose deaths are covered across multiple outlets and platforms.

Sources

For this component we draw on three primary sources, each of which contributes a different angle on nationally visible deaths:

  • Wikipedia lists of deaths by year
    Used to construct a baseline list of “celebrities” or nationally notable figures with Wikipedia pages who died within specified years. This provides a structured, date-stamped roster of individuals to search for obituary coverage.
  • WikiTree
    Used to explore selected individuals’ family trees, kinship ties, and sometimes linked obituaries or memorial pages. WikiTree helps contextualize the social and familial networks around specific well-known figures.
  • National obituary outlets
    Includes national newspapers and major media outlets that publish obituaries for notable figures. These outlets are used to locate full-text obituary narratives once a name and date of death are known.

All compiled celebrity obituary data and intermediate files are stored in the relevant Teams folder rather than on this public-facing site.

Linking strategy

The core idea is to start from structured lists of deceased notable individuals, then work outward to connect those names to obituary texts and, where possible, to kinship structures.

First, Wikipedia lists of deaths by year are used to identify individuals with Wikipedia pages who died within a selected time window. These lists are filtered to the years of interest for the project, and then used to assemble a working list of “dead celebrities.” From that list, we search for obituary coverage across national outlets and other public sources, using the names (and years of death) as anchors.

Second, for a subset of these individuals, we follow their names into WikiTree. This allows us to retrieve or inspect family trees, kinship ties, and cross-referenced memorial information. In some cases, WikiTree profiles link directly to obituary texts or provide enough structured detail (dates, locations, relationships) to help confirm that a candidate obituary corresponds to the correct person.

Finally, we use WikiTree in conjunction with the Wikipedia-derived lists and obituary searches to move from “name on a list” to “name with an attached obituary and network context.” This workflow is still being refined, and the exact rules for when and how WikiTree can be used as a systematic bridge from names to obituaries (needs further clarification and documentation internally).

Notes

The celebrity obituary component is meant to complement, not replace, the local and regional obituary sets from Georgia and Pennsylvania. It highlights how deaths of highly visible figures are narrated across national venues, which can be compared to more ordinary lives documented in local newspapers and funeral programs.

As the project progresses, we may refine inclusion criteria for “celebrity” status, expand or narrow the time window, and standardize how links between Wikipedia, WikiTree, and specific obituary texts are recorded.

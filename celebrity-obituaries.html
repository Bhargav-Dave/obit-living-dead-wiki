Celebrity obituaries

The celebrity obituary set provides a complementary perspective to local and regional obituaries. It focuses on nationally visible figures whose deaths are covered across multiple outlets and platforms, allowing us to compare patterns of commemoration at the local and national levels.

Sources

We rely on three main types of sources for celebrity deaths and their obituaries:

  • Wikipedia lists – lists of notable deaths by year, limited to individuals with Wikipedia pages.
  • WikiTree – collaborative genealogical profiles linked to those individuals.
  • National outlets – news organizations and magazines that regularly publish obituaries for prominent figures (for example, major national newspapers and magazines).

Methods

We use Wikipedia lists of notable deaths to create a roster of individuals with pages who died within a specified range of years. These lists provide initial information about names, dates, and broad domains of notoriety.

Using that roster, we search for obituaries through news outlets and other online repositories, then tag and consolidate the resulting obits into separate sets for Pennsylvania and Georgia when possible. This step helps bridge national coverage with state-level or local locations mentioned in the obituaries.

For a subset of individuals, we then use WikiTree to explore kinship ties, extended family networks, and additional memorial information. In some cases, we experiment with using WikiTree more broadly—starting from lists of names and working backward to locate obituaries via linked resources and references (this workflow still needs further clarification and documentation).

Data location

All intermediate datasets, cleaned lists, and codebooks for the celebrity obituary work are stored in the relevant Teams folder, alongside notes on coverage, inclusion criteria, and unresolved edge cases.

For more information on how this data is processed, see the Data Processing page.

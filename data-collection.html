Data Collection

The data collection module coordinates how we acquire obituary texts and related metadata from a variety of sources, including digitized newspapers, online archives, and funeral program collections. The goal is to assemble a transparent, reproducible corpus that can support both qualitative and computational analysis.

Source pages

Use these cards to jump into working notes for each major data source. Each page can track scraping logic, filters, and open questions. Fully collected datasets have a greenish tint.

Legacy.com

Sampled obituaries for Georgia and Pennsylvania, 2000–2024, using a month-based sampling strategy.

African American obituaries

Targeted collections from funeral programs, cemetery projects, and dedicated African American archives.

General newspapers

State and national newspaper archives used for regionally focused obituary collections.

Celebrity obituaries

Nationally visible figures whose deaths are covered in multiple outlets and platforms.

Other potential sources

We have also identified several other possible sources that could complement the main obituary corpus:

  • Family trees and kinship links (e.g., WikiTree)
  • Life-story repositories and memorial websites
  • Death certificates and vital records
  • Belated obituaries and events held in memoriam
  • Historical events and commemorative projects

We have not yet developed systematic methods for incorporating these sources; they remain part of an ongoing brainstorming list for future work.

For more information on how this data is processed, visit the data processing page.

Overview

This component brings together African American obituary sources from funeral programs, dedicated cemetery and obituary projects, library collections, and related web resources. The aim is to assemble curated sets that foreground Black communities while keeping source coverage and limitations transparent for the research team.

Sources

Below is a working list of core sources and collections. This list is intentionally broader than what is currently scraped and will be updated as we refine priorities.

  • Digital Library of Georgia's Funeral Programs
    Digital collections of African American funeral programs from Georgia communities, hosted by the Digital Library of Georgia.
    https://dlg.usg.edu/records?q=african+american+funeral+programs&search_field=both
  • African-American Cemeteries and Obituaries (BlackCemeteries.com)
    A site dedicated to African American cemeteries and obituary records, including searchable name-based obituary entries.
    https://www.blackcemeteries.com/?Inc=obituaries&Type=Name&String=Y
  • Georgia Southern Commons (Georgia Southern University)
    Digitized obituary collections (including African American materials) within the Georgia Southern University institutional repository.
    https://digitalcommons.georgiasouthern.edu/willowhillheritage-obituaries/
  • African American Funeral Programs (Houston Public Library Digital Archives)
    Digital funeral program collections from Houston Public Library, focusing on African American communities. (Exact collection link to be confirmed and added here.)
  • African American Funeral Programs from the Augusta-Richmond County Public Library System
    A curated collection of African American funeral programs from Augusta-Richmond County, hosted via the Digital Library of Georgia.
    https://dlg.usg.edu/collection/dlg_fpro
  • University of Georgia collection(s)
    Relevant obituary- and funeral-program-related holdings at UGA that include African American materials. Specific collections and call numbers are being catalogued internally.
  • Georgia Fulton County Library System
    Local news and obituary materials (not exclusively African American) that may intersect with Black communities and institutions. Currently used mainly as contextual background and supplementary obituary leads.
  • The Alford American Family Association
    Family association obituary compilations with entries specific to Georgia and Pennsylvania.
    Georgia: http://www.alfordassociation.org/OBITS/GAOBIT.htm
    Pennsylvania: http://www.alfordassociation.org/OBITS/PAOBIT.htm
  • ProQuest Black Newspapers dataset
    A licensed dataset of Black newspapers in which we have identified obituary clippings that require additional OCR and text cleaning before integration. These clippings extend coverage beyond open web and library-only sources and will be merged with other Black newspaper samples once processed.
  • Specific Black newspapers from state archives
    Within the Georgia state archives, several Black newspapers are already tagged and can be selectively targeted for obituary scraping. For Pennsylvania, we maintain curated lists of historically Black newspapers to approximate similar coverage even where archive tagging is incomplete.

Many of these sources are at different stages (scoping, sampling, active scraping). Detailed harvest logs and intermediate files live in the relevant Teams folder rather than on this site.

Our data sources / obit viewers

Most working data files for African American obituaries are stored in the relevant Teams folder. In addition, we maintain a set of exploratory “obit viewers” (Shiny apps) that help us inspect coverage, test filters, and spot systematic gaps. The descriptions below are provisional and should be verified against the latest internal documentation.

  • Black Cemeteries only obit finder
    A Shiny app that focuses on obituary and burial records linked specifically to Black cemeteries indexed on BlackCemeteries.com. Useful for quickly scanning name distributions and checking which cemeteries or time periods are well represented.
    https://newbruns19.shinyapps.io/blackcemeteries_cemeterieslist_shinyapp/
  • African American Obituary Finder
    A Shiny app that focuses on obituary entries from BlackCemeteries.com and related sources, designed to make it easier to search by name, date, and location. This tool is used to locate candidate obituaries that can then be followed up in more detail.
    https://newbruns19.shinyapps.io/blackcemeteriescom_shinyapp/

Additional viewers and dashboards are likely to be added as the project grows, especially for funeral-program-based collections and ProQuest-derived samples.

Notes

This page is meant to function as a living checklist of African American obituary sources, rather than a finalized inventory. As coverage improves, we may add more granular notes about temporal ranges, geographic focus, and which collections have usable full text versus image-only scans.

When in doubt, internal Teams documentation and scraping notebooks should be treated as the authoritative record of what has actually been collected and processed.

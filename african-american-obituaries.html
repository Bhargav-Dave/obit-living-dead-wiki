Overview

This component of the project focuses on African American obituaries and funeral memorials collected from a variety of digital repositories. The aim is to complement mainstream newspaper coverage with materials that center Black communities, institutions, and commemorative practices.

Many of these collections are structured as funeral programs, cemetery records, or family-compiled memorial lists rather than traditional newspaper death notices, which shapes both the language and the kinds of social ties that can be observed.

Sources

We compile African American obituary-like material from multiple institutional and community sources. Key examples include:

  • Digital Library of Georgia – Funeral Programs: https://dlg.usg.edu/records?q=african+american+funeral+programs&search_field=both
  • African-American Cemeteries and Obituaries (BlackCemeteries.com): https://www.blackcemeteries.com/?Inc=obituaries&Type=Name&String=Y
  • Georgia Southern Commons – Willow Hill Heritage Obituaries (Georgia Southern University): https://digitalcommons.georgiasouthern.edu/willowhillheritage-obituaries/
  • African American Funeral Programs (Houston Public Library Digital Archives): (link and access details to be documented).
  • African American Funeral Programs from the Augusta-Richmond County Public Library System: https://dlg.usg.edu/collection/dlg_fpro
  • University of Georgia collections: various obituary and funeral program holdings, to be inventoried.
  • Georgia Fulton County Library System: selected news and obituary materials, with an emphasis on African American coverage where available.
  • The Alford American Family Association (AAFA):
    Georgia: http://www.alfordassociation.org/OBITS/GAOBIT.htm
    Pennsylvania: http://www.alfordassociation.org/OBITS/PAOBIT.htm

Additional Archival Sources

Beyond the repositories above, we are also incorporating or planning to incorporate:

  • ProQuest Black Newspapers dataset: contains obituary clippings and death notices that require OCR extraction and additional cleaning.
  • Specific Black newspapers in state archives: in Georgia, many such titles are already tagged; in Pennsylvania, similar titles have been curated but may not yet be consistently flagged in the metadata. These curated lists will guide targeted scraping.

Together, these sources are intended to capture commemorative practices that might not be visible in mainstream or majority-white newspapers.

Our Data Sources & Obituary Viewers

Most of the curated data and intermediate files are present in the relevant Teams folder. To help navigate and explore these collections, we also rely on internal “obit viewer” tools built in Shiny. The descriptions below reflect current understanding and may need verification.

  • Black Cemeteries only obit finder: https://newbruns19.shinyapps.io/blackcemeteries_cemeterieslist_shinyapp/
    Used to browse cemetery-specific records and identify obituary-like entries tied to African American burial grounds.
  • African American Obituary Finder: https://newbruns19.shinyapps.io/blackcemeteriescom_shinyapp/
    Helps surface individual obituaries and related records drawn from BlackCemeteries.com and associated databases.

Note: source coverage, field definitions, and viewer behavior should be rechecked as these tools and collections are updated.

For more information on how this data is processed, visit the Data Processing & Design page.

Notes & Next Steps

Additional sources are likely to be added as we learn more about existing collections and uncover new repositories. Future work includes:

  • Developing standardized scraping and OCR workflows for each repository.
  • Tracking coverage by geography, denomination, and institution.
  • Aligning naming and metadata conventions across funeral programs, cemetery records, and newspaper obituaries.

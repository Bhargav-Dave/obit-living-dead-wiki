Overview

This page documents African American–focused obituary and funeral program sources used in the project. Because materials are dispersed across many local and thematic collections, we are compiling a working inventory of digital repositories, community archives, and genealogical resources relevant to Georgia and Pennsylvania.

Sources

We are compiling African American obituary and funeral program materials from a range of digital libraries, community projects, and specialized genealogical resources. The list below is a starting inventory and will expand as new collections are identified.

  • Digital Library of Georgia – Funeral Programs
    URL: https://dlg.usg.edu/records?q=african+american+funeral+programs&search_field=both Large, curated set of African American funeral programs from Georgia, with rich biographical narratives and family information.
  • African-American Cemeteries and Obituaries (blackcemeteries.com)
    URL: https://www.blackcemeteries.com/?Inc=obituaries&Type=Name&String=Y Community-driven index of African American cemetery and obituary records, used both as a direct data source and as a reference for cross-checking names and dates.
  • Georgia Southern Commons – Willow Hill Heritage Obituaries
    URL: https://digitalcommons.georgiasouthern.edu/willowhillheritage-obituaries/ Digitized obituaries and memorial materials associated with the Willow Hill Heritage collections, with strong coverage of local African American communities.
  • African American Funeral Programs (Houston Public Library Digital Archives)
    URL: TBA — see Teams for the exact collection link. Funeral program collection hosted by the Houston Public Library; relevant for comparative coverage and template design, even if not directly in Georgia or Pennsylvania.
  • African American Funeral Programs – Augusta-Richmond County Public Library System
    URL: https://dlg.usg.edu/collection/dlg_fpro Funeral programs for African Americans in Augusta-Richmond County, providing detailed life histories and kinship information for Georgia-based families.
  • University of Georgia Collection
    URL: TBA — specific obituary-related sub-collections to be catalogued. Umbrella reference for obituary-relevant holdings associated with the University of Georgia and partner repositories.
  • Georgia Fulton County Library System – Newspaper holdings
    URL: TBA — consult Teams for catalog links and access notes. Local news and obituary content for Fulton County and Atlanta-area publications, used to complement statewide and national sources.
  • The Alford American Family Association
    Genealogical association with compiled obituary indexes by state; relevant as a supplemental surname-based resource.
    • Georgia: http://www.alfordassociation.org/OBITS/GAOBIT.htm
    • Pennsylvania: http://www.alfordassociation.org/OBITS/PAOBIT.htm

Our data sources / obit viewers

Most underlying data files live in the relevant Teams folder. For exploratory browsing and quality checks, we also use the following Shiny-based obituary viewers (descriptions here are provisional and should be verified).

  • Black Cemeteries–only Obit Finder
    https://newbruns19.shinyapps.io/blackcemeteries_cemeterieslist_shinyapp/ Prototype viewer focused on cemetery-level records from blackcemeteries.com, allowing users to explore burials and associated obituaries by cemetery and location.
  • African American Obituary Finder
    https://newbruns19.shinyapps.io/blackcemeteriescom_shinyapp/ Viewer centered on obituary-level entries derived from blackcemeteries.com, designed to support search by name, date, and location.

Note: The scope and underlying data flow for these viewers should be confirmed against the latest code and documentation in Teams.

Notes

This page is a living inventory. Additional African American obituary and funeral program sources will be added as we identify further digital collections, local library initiatives, and community archives in Georgia, Pennsylvania, and beyond.

When adding a new source, include a stable URL (where possible), a one- or two-sentence description, and a short note indicating how it connects to the core Pennsylvania/Georgia obituary work.

For more information on how this data is processed, visit the Data Processing page.

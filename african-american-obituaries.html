African American obituaries

This page tracks targeted collections of African American obituaries and memorial materials across multiple archives and platforms. Many of these sources are local, specialized, or fragile, and often require bespoke collection strategies beyond standard newspaper scraping.

Sources

We are compiling a heterogeneous set of collections that contain African American obituaries, funeral programs, and related materials:

  • Digital Library of Georgia – Funeral programs: https://dlg.usg.edu/records?q=african+american+funeral+programs&search_field=both
  • African-American Cemeteries and Obituaries: https://www.blackcemeteries.com/?Inc=obituaries&Type=Name&String=Y
  • Georgia Southern Commons (Georgia Southern University) – Willow Hill Heritage obituaries: https://digitalcommons.georgiasouthern.edu/willowhillheritage-obituaries/
  • African American Funeral Programs (Houston Public Library Digital Archives) – link and coverage details to be finalized.
  • African American Funeral Programs from the Augusta-Richmond County Public Library System: https://dlg.usg.edu/collection/dlg_fpro
  • University of Georgia collections – relevant obituary and funeral-program holdings (specific series to be catalogued).
  • Georgia Fulton County Library System – primarily news content with obituary coverage in select local titles.
  • The Alford American Family Association – surname-based obituary collections:
    • Georgia: http://www.alfordassociation.org/OBITS/GAOBIT.htm
    • Pennsylvania: http://www.alfordassociation.org/OBITS/PAOBIT.htm
  • ProQuest Black Newspapers dataset – obituary clippings that require additional OCR and text-cleaning steps.
  • Black newspapers in state archives – curated lists of historically Black titles in Georgia (tagged) and Pennsylvania (work in progress).

Obit viewers & internal data

Consolidated datasets and intermediate files are stored in the relevant Teams folder. Two prototype Shiny apps provide searchable interfaces for subsets of these sources (descriptions below still need verification):

  • Black Cemeteries only obit finder – https://newbruns19.shinyapps.io/blackcemeteries_cemeterieslist_shinyapp/
  • African American Obituary Finder – https://newbruns19.shinyapps.io/blackcemeteriescom_shinyapp/

These tools are primarily for exploratory browsing and data-quality checks; final research datasets will be exported and documented separately.

Notes

This collection is intentionally open-ended. Additional funeral-program collections, cemetery datasets, and community archives will be added as they are identified and evaluated for coverage and feasibility.

For more information on how this data is processed, see the Data Processing page.
